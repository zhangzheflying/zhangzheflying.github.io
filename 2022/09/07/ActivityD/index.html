Research objectives 4

Modelling the long-term impacts of fertilization by inorganic and organic fertilizer on soil acidification rates

VSD+ (Very Simple Dynamic Model) Model Structure

The dynamic soil acidification model, VSD+, has been developed to predict and quantify long-term acidification trends in soils by calculating input-output budgets. This work will be expanded by calibrating VSD+ results on measurements of pH and exchangeable BC in the 13 long-term experimental sites. The effect of manure application to changes in SOC and CEC will also be included, requiring an extension/ update of the current VSD+ model. Data on the CEC and exchangeable cations at the start and at the end of the experimental plots will be used to assess relationships between (i) pH and exchangeable base saturation and (ii) exchangeable Al saturation and exchangeable base saturation. This is relevant for evaluating the validity of the VSD+ model, together with a comparison of the modelled and measured acidification history (pH reconstruction). Additionally, VSD+ will be used to predict future changes in response to fertilizer and management options.
