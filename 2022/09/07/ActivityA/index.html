Research objective 1

Long-term impacts of inorganic and organic fertilizer inputs on nitrogen use efficiency in rice wheat, maize and soybean systems in China for different site conditions

Abstract figure

Increasing the NUE for given site properties is an important approach to balance the N supply and demand thus combining the need for enhanced food supply with environmental protection. We assess the impact of management (crop type, fertilizer rate, fertilizer type), climate (precipitation, temperature, and sunshine hours) and soil properties (e.g., soil type, contents of soil organic matter, SOM, total or available contents of N, P, and K) on the NUE using multi variate (non-linear) regression and machine learning algorithms by collecting data from 13 long-term experiments. This gives us insights in the driving factors as well as options for further optimization of N fertilization strategies.
