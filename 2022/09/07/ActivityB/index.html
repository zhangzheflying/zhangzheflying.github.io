Research objectives 2

Soil acidification and driving factors under long-term nitrogen fertilizer application in Southern China

Input and output balance of nitrogen and base Cation (BC)

Soil acidification is the production of protons, resulting in an increase in soil acidity, manifested as a decrease in soil pH. A decline in soil pH depends on N surplus, BC (including potassium (K+), calcium (Ca2+), and magnesium (Mg2+)) surplus, and soil acid buffer system itself. This study/paper aims to relate the decrease in soil pH in long-term experiments to the nitrogen fertilizer type and rate, crop yield (base cation uptake) and soil properties affecting the soil N transformations and soil acid buffer capacity (organic matter content, clay content and pH). So, systematic research of the role of the driving factors controlling the pH decline, including the N surplus, BC surplus and CEC under long-term fertilizer and manure application strategies will be done.
